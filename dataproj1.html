Data Science Portfolio Project at Macquarie University

This data science portfolio was developed as part of the COMP2200/6200 coursework (S1 2022) at Macquarie University, where I achieved a high distinction. It includes analyses of the "MovieLens" dataset from GroupLens Research and the "StudentsPerformance" dataset from Kaggle, demonstrating core data science skills such as data cleaning, exploratory analysis, feature selection, predictive modeling, and evaluation methodologies. Techniques applied include regression (linear, logistic, polynomial), classification, clustering, and machine learning algorithms like K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN). Throughout, I emphasized using proper tools and techniques to solve data-driven problems, alongside detailed visualizations and analyses that underscore the practical applications and outcomes of these tasks.

This portfolio, organized into four sections and enriched with reflective insights, showcases a comprehensive understanding of data science fundamentals, including graphical data displays, advanced model evaluation, and actionable recommendations. Due to academic integrity restrictions posed by Macquarie University, the repository remains private; however, it is available to potential recruiters upon request from here. This body of work highlights my analytical abilities and problem-solving approach, reflecting skills that are valuable in real-world data science and business contexts.
